# Decision: MedBrief EMI employee share option scheme adopted

## Decision

MedBrief will run a HMRC-registered Enterprise Management Incentive (EMI) scheme as the vehicle for its Group Equity Incentive Scheme. Employees receive cashless share options in Lex Vision Holdings (the ultimate holding company), with a base award of 10% of prior-year gross remuneration priced at 81p per share, and an exercise target of combined annual revenue growth and EBITDA summing to 40. Announced and set out as made decisions by Brendan Hughes at the Q1 company update on 2026-03-26.

## Rationale

The EMI structure delivers capital gains tax treatment rather than PAYE for UK tax residents on exercise, which is the most efficient route for staff. HMRC qualification rules require the options to sit in the ultimate holding company (Lex Vision Holdings), which also puts option holders at the same level as the founders. The cashless exercise structure fully de-risks the option for employees: there is no upfront cost and any share price below the 81p strike is irrelevant to them, so the only possible outcome is upside. The exercise target was designed to be self-reinforcing, since hitting the combined revenue-growth-plus-EBITDA threshold of 40 is also what drives the share price above the strike.

## Downstream impact

- Brendan Hughes owns the rollout: issuing individual option letters, arranging sessions for non-UK tax resident staff, submitting documents to HMRC, holding follow-up Q&A sessions, and reporting quarterly on share price tracking.
- Commits the group's equity structure and ties roughly 100 employees into ownership.
- Establishes a recurring quarterly reporting obligation on share price performance.
