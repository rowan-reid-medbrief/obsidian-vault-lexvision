---

## Purpose

Kick-off discussion for the MedBrief company-wide data strategy initiative. This is a separate track from the clinical pipeline / Kumulus POC (which is the LexVision data engineering work). This covers the full business: how MedBrief captures, stores, and uses its data across every part of the organisation.

---

## Context and current state

MedBrief's data landscape currently has several pain points:

- **Silos:** data lives in separate systems with no unified view across the business.
- **Non-usable formats:** data is not consistently structured or accessible for analysis or automation.
- **Fragmented workflows:** processes that depend on data are manual or disconnected.

The goal is to move from this state to a defined, implemented data strategy that covers the whole business.

---

## Scope

The strategy should cover MedBrief's three core cycles:

- **Customer cycle** — how customer/client data flows through the business.
- **Product cycle** — data generated by and needed to operate the product.
- **People cycle** — internal HR, team, and operational data.

A first concrete step is to **identify all data sources** across the various systems the business runs on.

---

## Approach

Laura's current thinking is to bring in a specialist third-party partner (likely a Microsoft partner or data engineering consultancy) to help define the strategy and potentially lead implementation. The extent of internal involvement from each team member will evolve as the project takes shape.

Kumulus (already engaged on the LexVision clinical pipeline) was raised as a possible partner, or a similar type of firm. The work is expected to be substantial, involving data migration and record processing.

**Next steps on partner research:**
- Research specialist third-party options (look at what similar organisations have done).
- Azure / Microsoft is a likely platform anchor; engage their CSA (Customer Success Architect) programme as a route to the right contacts.
- Meeting with **Dot Collective** already scheduled (see below).
