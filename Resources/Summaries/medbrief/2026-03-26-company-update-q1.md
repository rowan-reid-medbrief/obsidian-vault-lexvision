# MedBrief Q1 Company Update (March 2026) Summary

## Context

This was MedBrief's quarterly company update call for Q1 of the 2025/2026 financial year, held in March 2026. The agenda ran through financial performance (Matthew Harrison), operational performance and HR (Steve Ashford), the new group equity incentive scheme (Brendan Hughes), technology, IT and AI (Deon Kuhn and colleagues), marketing and client engagement (Mike Lewis, covering for Jess on maternity leave), and closing CEO remarks (Adam Stead). The two headline items were the launch of an employee share option scheme and the launch of MedBrief Match, the company's first client-facing AI feature.

## Key points

- Revenue rebounded after the loss of Shoesmiths to an acquisition, and the company is back on a growth track. The headline growth rate dropped from the 20s to around 10%, but this is a comparison artefact: last year's figure was inflated by a one-off push to clear a backlog, which has now rolled out of the year-on-year comparison.
- Costs are under control and profit before tax has recovered. Steve reported an average sorting turnaround of 6 days against a 7-day target.
- The Q1 performance bonus pool was confirmed at £11,609.04, payable in December.
- The Group Equity Incentive Scheme was launched: a HMRC-registered EMI scheme giving employees cashless share options in Lex Vision Holdings (the ultimate holding company). Base award is 10% of prior-year gross remuneration, priced at 81p per share, with an exercise target of revenue growth plus EBITDA summing to 40.
- MedBrief Match launched as the company's first client-facing AI feature. It helps legal teams find the right medical expert quickly, with AI specialty recommendations, a natural-language reason for each suggested expert, and a letter-of-approach generator. It is already live and piloting with LIME Solicitors, and represents over 1,000 hours of work from around 15 people across the AI and development teams.
- Five new law firm clients came on board in the quarter (Chadwick Lawrence, Setfords, Osbornes, Lindsays, Hay & Kilner), including a first move into the Scottish legal market, which differs meaningfully from England and Wales.
- Adam Stead closed with a reflection on the company's mission, using the story of Andrew Jenkins (a brain injury survivor) to illustrate the human impact, and framed the equity scheme as making roughly 100 employees practically, not just symbolically, part-owners of the business.

## Decisions and positions

Brendan Hughes set out the full design of the equity scheme as a set of made decisions: the EMI vehicle, issuing options in Lex Vision Holdings, the 10%-of-salary base award at an 81p strike, the cashless exercise structure, and the combined revenue-growth-plus-EBITDA target of 40. The rationale throughout was tax efficiency for UK staff (capital gains rather than PAYE treatment), HMRC qualification rules, and fully de-risking the option for employees so the only possible outcome is upside above the strike price.

Chantel van Wyk presented MedBrief Match as a launch-day milestone rather than a proposal, describing it as a big and proud moment for the company. Deon Kuhn confirmed automated testing for Match as the first QA automation priority and flagged GP Sorting Sally improvements going to production within a couple of weeks.
