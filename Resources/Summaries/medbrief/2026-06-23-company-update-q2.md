# MedBrief Q2 Company Update (June 2026) Summary

## Context

MedBrief's quarterly company update for Q2, held in June 2026. The agenda ran through finance (Matthew Harrison), operations and HR including a clinical team restructure (Steve Ashford), the practical rollout of the Group Equity Incentive Scheme (Brendan Hughes), a product update on MedBrief Match and Insights (Chantel van Wyk), AI initiatives (Laura Goncas), the technology update including a new development AI agent (Deon Kuhn), marketing and sales (Mike Lewis), and closing CEO remarks on the growth vision (Adam Stead). This is the follow-on quarter to the March update, where the equity scheme and MedBrief Match were first launched.

## Key points

- A good but not stellar quarter. Revenue up 7.8% year-on-year, Group EBITDA 16.1%, financial performance score tracking at 24% year to date against the rule-of-40 target. Costs slightly higher, partly from increased borrowing.
- MedBrief completed its ISO 27001 and 9001 re-qualification.
- The clinical sorting team was restructured: pods merged, the departed team-leader responsibilities redistributed, and a clearer progression route and training structure introduced. The chronology team is next for the same treatment.
- The equity scheme moved from announcement to practical rollout. The valuation is now set: Lex Vision Holdings holds 56.72% of the group, valued at £8.1m on a 3.45 revenue multiple, with options struck at 81p. Realization events pay out 75% immediately and retain 25% with interest through a retention period. Everyone has the same FY27 rule-of-40 performance target.
- Product: MedBrief Match is expanding beyond LIME to Gatsby, Wix and Switalski (19 experts have sent letters of approach since launch). MedBrief Insights is heading for a July pilot. A records-request automation exercise on the Ryland Scott class action (Emily with JD) saved the ops team around 100 hours.
- The development team unveiled its own AI agent, Athena, which has catalogued MedBrief's entire development infrastructure and codebase and will help onboard future developers. The chatting-with-records proof of value returned positive feedback, and AI performance dashboards are now company-wide.
- Marketing had its busiest quarter: new clients (Trethowans, Boyes Turner, Hugh James, Setfords), conferences and events, the SIA Overseas Ride raising over £2,100, and a well-received podcast series. LinkedIn engagement and website SEO both up.
- Adam Stead closed on the growth story: 7.4x revenue growth since 2018, matched exactly by 7.4x headcount growth, with gross profit outpacing both. The strategic goal is now to grow value without growing headcount at the same rate, through tooling, adoption and training.

## Decisions and positions

Brendan Hughes set out the equity scheme rollout as a series of made decisions: the valuation and 81p strike, the realization-event split (75% immediate, 25% retained with interest), and a single FY27 rule-of-40 target for all staff. He answered two staff questions directly: why options are in Lex Vision Holdings rather than MedBrief (UK tax rules require the ultimate holding company), and whether 81p is fair (he considers it fair and reasonable, derived from revenue and an independent multiple).

Steve Ashford presented the clinical sorting restructure as a decision already taken, with the chronology team to follow. Chantel van Wyk framed the MedBrief Insights July pilot and the Match expansion as planned next steps. Adam Stead set the strategic direction for the year: add value without simply adding headcount, and invest in tools while committing the whole company to actually using them.
